Gyros

Description

Gyros is a delicious lunch or dinner meal consisting of meat stuffed in pita bread with other ingredients to your liking. The meal developed from döner kebab in the 19th century Ottoman Empire. It made its way to Athens after World War II, where the Greek variant gyros, made with lamb and tzatziki, was born.

In two hours or less, you can cook four servings of mouth-watering gyros that you and your guests will love.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup red onion
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon dried marjoram
  • 1 tablespoon ground dried rosemary
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pound 93%-lean ground beef
  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 4 pita bread rounds
  • 8 tablespoons tzatziki
  • 2 ounces crumbled feta cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 °F.
  2. Chop red onion, then finely mince in a food processor.
  3. Scoop onion into a cheesecloth piece. Squeeze to remove as much moisture as possible.
  4. Place onion back in food processor; add garlic then process until mixed well.
  5. Blend in marjoram, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  6. Add alternating small amounts of beef and lamb to onion mixture. Process each batch thoroughly until moving onto the next.
  7. Pack mixture into 9x5-inch loaf pan. Assure no air pockets are trapped in meat.
  8. Bake 1 hour 15 minutes or until center is at least 175 °F and no longer pink. Drain grease, then slice thinly.
  9. Onto each pita bread, place a tablespoon of hummus, meat, feta, and 2 tablespoons tzatziki. Serve.
